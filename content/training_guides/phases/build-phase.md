# Phase 2: Build (Weeks 5-10)

{ATHLETE_NAME}, the build phase layers intensity and develops race-specific fitness.

## Training Characteristics

- 60-70% of volume in Z1-Z2
- 30-40% in Z3-Z6 (tempo, threshold, VO2max)
- Two to three quality sessions per week
- Long rides now include race-specific work (surges, climbs, tempo blocks)
- Recovery weeks every 3-4 weeks (Week 8)

## What's Happening Physiologically

- FTP increases (threshold power improves)
- VO2max rises (aerobic ceiling lifts)
- Lactate clearance improves
- Muscular power and repeatability develop
- Race-specific adaptations occur

## Training Load Metrics During Build

- **Weekly TSS:** Higher and more variable (400-800+ depending on category)
- **Hard weeks:** 600-1000 TSS, ATL spikes, TSB goes negative (-20 to -40)
- **Recovery weeks:** 300-500 TSS, ATL drops, TSB recovers to -5 to -10
- **CTL:** Continues rising but more gradually (+2-4 points per week)
- **Pattern:** Yo-yo between stress accumulation and recovery

## The Build Phase Pattern

This is where the magic happens. You deliberately dig a fatigue hole (TSB goes very negative during hard weeks), then recover just enough to absorb the adaptation (TSB normalizes during recovery weeks), then dig another hole.

- Week 5-6: Push hard, accumulate fatigue (TSB = -25)
- Week 7: Recover (TSB = -10)
- Week 8: Recovery week (TSB = -5)
- Week 9-10: Push harder, accumulate more fatigue (TSB = -35)

## How It Feels

Hard. Quality sessions should hurt. You'll question whether you can complete intervals. That's normal.

Easy days should still be easy. If everything feels hard, you're overtraining.

## Common Mistakes

- Turning easy days into tempo rides
- Adding extra intervals because you feel good
- Skipping recovery weeks because you want to keep pushing
- Ignoring fatigue signals (TSB below -40 for extended periods)
